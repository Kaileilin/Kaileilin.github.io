Figure.2. This figure plots the location quotient (LQ) for industries in Auburn in 2016 and 2021. A location quotient is a statistic representing the degree of concentration of employment in a location. An LQ greater than 1 implies more people are employed in that industry than the city’s population would lead one to expect, while an LQ less than 1 implies there are fewer people employed than the city’s population would expect. The LQ also provides insight on whether the city “exports” or “imports” goods and services in a particular industry: LQs greater than 1 imply the city exports that industry’s goods and/or services, and an LQ less than 1 implies the city imports these instead. We can gain understanding into a city’s economic role within a regional economy by looking at its LQs in various industries.

Over the 2016 - 2021 period, Auburn experienced a transition in its employment composition and LQ. The city saw shifts away from primary or upstream industries (Agriculture, Manufacturing, Logistics, and Wholesale Trade) and toward more service-oriented industries such as Administration, Information, Hospitality, and Professional Services.

In 2021, City of Auburn also saw construction becoming outsized, growing to provide services for the greater region. Specifically, Auburn leds the the rest of San Diego County in Serveral industries including Agriculutre, retail Trade, Wholesale Trade and Other services except public administration.
In 2021, Auburn witnessed substantial employment growth in Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing, and Mining, following greater-than-average job losses during the previous year's pandemic-related layoffs. On the other hand, most other industries in Auburn experienced more moderate changes in employment, mirroring the trends seen across California as a whole. This entailed a decline in employment in Wholesale Trade, Arts, Entertainment, and Hospitality, alongside notable growth in Logistics and Construction.

Interestingly, despite a statewide decrease in retail employment, Auburn actually saw year-over-year growth in this sector. Overall, the city of Auburn witnessed more pronounced fluctuations in employment due to its smaller employment base compared to the state as a whole.
